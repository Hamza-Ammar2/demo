LAYER 02 — MORE INTERESTING PREDICTION TARGETS
Dataset research memo (for Luke + teammate)
============================================

YOUR POINT (fair)
-----------------
Cycle-phase / period prediction is a real ML task, but Flo, Clue, Natural Cycles,
Oura, Apple, etc. already sell versions of it. For a hackathon Layer 02, "we
predict your period" is easy to dismiss unless the story is sharper
(hormone-verified labels + leakage audit + open reusable pipeline).

Better bar: predict something clinicians and researchers still struggle with,
where open data exists, and where "unsupported diagnostic claim" risk is managed.


THE RULE THAT STILL HOLDS
-------------------------
You can add any dataset you want — but only if it contains labels for the thing
you want to predict. More NHANES ≠ cysts. Apple/Clue big data ≠ downloadable.


========================================================================
TIER S — MOST INTERESTING + HACKATHON-REALISTIC
========================================================================

1) MENOPAUSE TRANSITION / HORMONAL AGING  —  SWAN (Study of Women's Health Across the Nation)
   What it is
     Multi-site US longitudinal study of midlife women (~3,300). Public-use files
     on ICPSR (baseline through Visit 10); fuller access via NIA Aging Research Biobank.
   Labels / targets you can actually train
     - Menopausal stage / transition (pre → early peri → late peri → post)
     - Hormone trajectories: E2, FSH, T, SHBG, DHEAS, AMH
     - Symptom burden across the transition (vasomotor, sleep, mood)
     - THIS is the real answer to challenge.txt's "early or late menopause" bullet
   Why it's more interesting than cycle phase
     Menopause timing / stage is clinically consequential, under-modeled in consumer
     apps, and explicitly named in the brief. Almost no Flo-clone does this well
     with open reproducible code.
   Access
     ICPSR public-use: relatively fast (account + accept terms). Ideal for students.
     Biobank: proposal + IRB/DUA — slower.
   Fit with our project
     Strong Layer 02. Weak direct plug into Sarah demo unless we add a "midlife"
     persona. Still excellent as the trained scientific asset.
   Risk
     Not "diagnose menopause in clinic from a wearable tomorrow" — stage/trajectory
     prediction with clear limits.


2) ENDOMETRIOSIS SYMPTOM DYNAMICS  —  KI EndoLIST (PhysioNet, restricted)
   What it is
     34 Israeli patients with confirmed endometriosis; daily individualized symptom
     tracking over months; MedDRA-mapped symptoms; high engagement.
     https://physionet.org/content/ki-endolist/1.0.0/
   Labels / targets
     NOT "do you have endometriosis?" (everyone already does).
     YES: next-day / next-week symptom flare severity, symptom co-occurrence,
     individualized burden phenotypes, cycle-linked pain patterns.
   Why interesting
     Diagnosis delay is 4–12 years; the unsolved problem is heterogeneous day-to-day
     burden and communication with clinicians — which is EXACTLY Case Compiler's
     product shape (timeline → doctor brief), but for a disease cohort.
   Access
     Same PhysioNet restricted path as mcPHASES (you already know how).
   Fit
     Best narrative upgrade to the APP: "Case Compiler for endometriosis flares."
     Model = flare / severity predictor; engine = provenance brief.
   Risk
     Small n (34). Must not claim diagnostic screening.


3) SEX-SPECIFIC DRUG–ECG RESPONSE  —  PhysioNet clinical pharmacology ECGs
   Datasets
     - ECGDMMLD: dofetilide/moxifloxacin ± other drugs; healthy M/F; PK + ECG features
       https://physionet.org/content/ecgdmmld/
     - ECGRDVQ: ranolazine, dofetilide, verapamil, quinidine vs placebo
       https://physionet.org/content/ecgrdvq/
   Labels / targets
     Predict ΔQTc / ECG feature change under drug, stratified by sex; or learn a
     cross-sex translator of drug-response ECG features (the real Grandi-lab idea).
   Why interesting
     Directly hits challenge bullet #3: "clinical trials where women's hormonal /
     sex biology affects efficacy or safety." Women have higher drug-induced
     torsades risk. Almost nobody in a women's-health hackathon ships this.
   Fit
     Strong Layer 02 science story; weaker tie to Case Compiler UI unless framed as
     "infra for sex-aware trial safety."
   Risk
     Small trials (~22 subjects each). Not hormonal-cycle data. Team must explain
     the bridge ("women's physiology → drug safety gap").


========================================================================
TIER A — INTERESTING, BUT QUALITY / CLAIM RISKS
========================================================================

4) PCOS RISK / SCREENING FROM CLINICAL FEATURES  —  Kaggle PCOS (~541 patients)
   Features: age, BMI, FSH, LH, AMH, follicle counts, symptoms (hirsutism, acne…).
   Target: PCOS Y/N.
   Why people like it: "disease prediction," explainable XGBoost, easy demo.
   Why be careful:
     - Recycled in dozens of student papers → judges may yawn or smell homework.
     - Follicle counts / ultrasound-derived features can make the task nearly
       circular (you're almost reading the diagnosis criteria back).
     - License / provenance of the Kaggle dump is often murky.
   Verdict: doable in a weekend; scientifically softer than SWAN / EndoLIST /
   drug-ECG unless you add a harsh external validation story.


5) PCOS / CYST FROM ULTRASOUND IMAGES  —  Kaggle / PCOSGen (Zenodo)
   Target: polycystic ovary appearance on US frames.
   Interesting clinically; totally different stack (CNNs, imaging).
   Weak glue to Case Compiler (text/timeline app). High risk of diagnostic framing.
   Verdict: only if the team wants an imaging side-quest, not the main bet.


6) IVF / EMBRYO OUTCOMES  —  mixed openness
   - Figshare Brigham oocyte/embryo anonymized clinical data (train/test split exists)
   - Larger ART time-lapse sets (often paper-associated; access varies)
   Targets: blastocyst formation, pregnancy, dose individualization.
   Very interesting; often messy access, clinical ethics sensitivity, and "we predict
   IVF success" is a landmine for unsupported claims.
   Verdict: stretch only.


========================================================================
TIER B — USEFUL SUPPORT DATA, NOT THE HEADLINE MODEL
========================================================================

7) MORE NHANES CYCLES
   Good for: bigger hormone reference ranges, metabolic context, older RHQ items
   (endometriosis self-report in some cycles), PCOS *proxy* features.
   Bad for: cyst imaging, true PCOS gold labels, longitudinal flares, menopause
   trajectories (cross-sectional).
   Keep as Layer 01 enrichment, not the Layer 02 hero task.


8) mcPHASES (already have)
   Best non-period targets on THIS data (more interesting than phase alone):
     - Next-day severe headache / cramp flare given wearables + sleep + phase
     - Wearable → hormone-metabolite *state* (noisy; frame carefully)
     - Glucose × cycle interactions (CGM is rare + cool)
   Still small n=42. Good as multimodal module, not as disease diagnosis.

========================================================================
RECOMMENDED PIVOTS (PICK ONE HEADLINE)
========================================================================

If you want "more interesting than period apps," pick ONE:

A. MENOPAUSE-STAGE / TRANSITION MODEL on SWAN (ICPSR)
   Pitch: "Open, reproducible model of menopausal transition from longitudinal
   hormones + symptoms — the gap consumer cycle apps don't cover."
   Closest match to challenge.txt Layer 02 bullet 1 (menopause).

B. ENDOMETRIOSIS FLARE / BURDEN MODEL on KI EndoLIST + Case Compiler UI
   Pitch: "Same app architecture, harder disease: predict and brief daily
   endometriosis symptom burden with provenance."
   Best product continuity with what you already built.

C. SEX-AWARE DRUG–ECG SAFETY MODEL on PhysioNet ECG trials
   Pitch: "Reusable model for sex differences in drug cardiac safety — Layer 02
   clinical-trial bullet, plus the real Cross-Sex Translator story."
   Best "wow, that's not another period app" science flex; weakest Sarah demo fit.

D. HYBRID (ambitious)
   Keep Case Compiler + mcPHASES multimodal flare/state model as the app,
   AND add SWAN menopause OR drug-ECG as the published Layer 02 checkpoint.
   Strongest "infrastructure" submission; more work.


WHAT I WOULD NOT BET THE TEAM ON
--------------------------------
- Ovarian cyst diagnosis from NHANES
- Another Kaggle PCOS classifier as the sole Layer 02 story (unless time-starved)
- Female→male conversion so AI can "diagnose"
- Claiming we beat Flo at period prediction


BOTTOM LINE
-----------
Yes — add datasets. The interesting open ones are not "more NHANES for cysts."
They are roughly:

  SWAN          → menopause / hormonal aging (public ICPSR)
  KI EndoLIST   → endometriosis flare dynamics (PhysioNet restricted)
  PhysioNet ECG drug trials → sex-specific safety / cross-sex response
  Kaggle PCOS   → easy but crowded / claim-risky
  US imaging    → cysts/PCOS appearance, different product

Cycle phase isn't "wrong." It's just table stakes. Layer 02 gets exciting when
the prediction target is something the market and the literature still lack an
open, auditable model for.
