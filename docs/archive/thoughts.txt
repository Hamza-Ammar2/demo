THOUGHTS ON THE CHALLENGE
=========================================================================
(Working notes — strategy, not commitments. Meant to help pick a lane.)

-------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. WHAT THE JUDGES ARE ACTUALLY REWARDING
-------------------------------------------------------------------------
Read the "Strong vs Weak" table as the real rubric. Three things dominate:

  (a) REUSABILITY. They say "reusable building block" and "reusable
      scientific asset" repeatedly. The winning artifact is something another
      researcher can `git clone` / `pip install` / download and use on Monday.
      An app with no reusable dataset/benchmark/model is explicitly called a
      WEAK submission.

  (b) ONE PROBLEM, DONE WELL. "Choose ONE specific hormone-related problem."
      Scope discipline is rewarded; a sprawling half-built foundation model is
      punished. Narrow + rigorous beats broad + shallow.

  (c) OPEN SCIENCE + HONESTY. Documented train/val/test splits, transparent
      preprocessing, no unsupported medical claims. This is a research-hygiene
      contest as much as an ML contest.

Implication: the safest high-ceiling play is Track 01 (Data & Benchmark),
optionally with a small Track 02 model as the "baseline" that ships with the
benchmark. That directly maximizes reusability and is the least crowded lane
(most hackathon teams reflexively build an app = Track 03).

-------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. HONEST READ OF THE TWO DATASETS WE HAVE
-------------------------------------------------------------------------
NHANES (already downloaded, cleaned to CSV in data/processed/):
  + Large n (10k-15k per cycle), nationally representative, weights available.
  + Rich sex-steroid panel: estradiol, estrone, SHBG, FSH, LH, progesterone,
    AMH, androstenedione, 17-OHP + demographics, body measures, biochem.
  + CROSS-SECTIONAL. One blood draw per person. Great for "given labs +
    demographics, predict X" but CANNOT show hormones-over-time.
  - Total testosterone is RDC-only for 2017+ (public only in 2013-2016 via
    TST_H / TST_I). Worth remembering if T is a target.
  - Menstrual-cycle phase is NOT directly measured; RIDEXPRG (pregnancy) and
    reproductive questionnaire (RHQ) exist but no daily cycle tracking.

mcPHASES (PhysioNet, restricted; download INCOMPLETE — see section 6):
  + LONGITUDINAL + MULTIMODAL: Fitbit (HR, temp, sleep, HRV, resp, SpO2,
    stress, activity), Dexcom CGM, daily Mira hormone metabolites (LH, E3G,
    PdG), and self-reported symptoms. 23 tables.
  + This is the star dataset for the challenge's "dynamic biology" theme —
    it's exactly the continuous hormone-vs-physiology signal they lament is
    missing.
  - SMALL n: 42 participants (20 with a 2nd 3-month round). Young Canadian
    adults, no hormonal contraceptives, no diabetes. Narrow demographic.
  - Restricted license (PhysioNet Restricted Health Data DUA). CANNOT be
    re-published openly. So our OPEN deliverable must be CODE + BENCHMARK
    DEFINITION + MODELS, not the raw data itself. Important for the open-
    science requirement: publish the pipeline, point to the gated source.

The two datasets are complementary, not substitutes:
  NHANES = breadth / population reference ranges / cross-sectional labs.
  mcPHASES = depth / temporal dynamics / wearable-to-hormone mapping.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. CANDIDATE PROJECTS (ranked by fit-to-rubric x feasibility-in-a-weekend)
-------------------------------------------------------------------------

*** TOP PICK — "WearHormone" benchmark: predict menstrual-cycle phase /
    hormone state from wearable signals (Track 01 + small Track 02 baseline).
  - Task: from Fitbit-derived daily features (resting HR, wrist temp, HRV,
    sleep, respiration), predict the Mira-labeled phase (menstruation, late-
    follicular, ovulation, luteal) and/or regress E3G/PdG/LH.
  - Deliverable: a clean, documented benchmark on mcPHASES with FIXED,
    published participant-level train/val/test splits (split by participant to
    avoid leakage — critical, and something most teams get wrong), an
    evaluation script, metrics (macro-F1 for phase, MAE for hormones), and 2-3
    baseline models (logistic reg / gradient boosting / small temporal model).
  - Why it wins: maximally reusable, directly targets the "no shared
    benchmark" pain point, honest about the tiny-n limitation, and the
    leakage-aware split IS the scientific contribution. Reproducible in a
    weekend.
  - Risk: n=42 means results are illustrative, not clinical. FRAME IT THAT WAY.
    The value is the benchmark + methodology, not SOTA accuracy.

  ALT — "HormoneNet reference ranges": open, reproducible NHANES pipeline that
    produces age/sex/BMI-stratified reference distributions for the sex-steroid
    panel + a baseline model predicting a hormone (e.g. SHBG or estradiol) from
    demographics + routine biochem.
  - Why: 100% openly publishable (NHANES is public domain), big n, no DUA
    friction. Great "data infrastructure" artifact. Less flashy than wearables
    but bulletproof on the open-science axis.
  - Could be the SAFE half of a two-part submission.

  STRETCH — Cross-dataset harmonization layer: a schema + loader that maps
    NHANES hormone variables and mcPHASES hormone metabolites into one
    common, documented data model with units normalized (NHANES gives SI
    conversion factors; mcPHASES gives Mira metabolite values). This is
    literally "the standardized multimodal dataset" bullet. High reuse value
    but more plumbing than modeling — good if the team likes data engineering.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. WHAT I'D AVOID
-------------------------------------------------------------------------
  - A polished symptom-tracker app with no benchmark/model behind it. Explicit
    WEAK example.
  - Any "diagnose PCOS / predict endometriosis" claim from this data. We do
    not have labeled disease outcomes at the fidelity needed; that's an
    "unsupported medical claim" trap.
  - A giant multimodal foundation model. Weekend-infeasible and against their
    own advice ("rather than attempting one large foundation model").
  - Training on mcPHASES with random row-level splits — guaranteed leakage
    across a person's own days; inflates metrics and kills credibility.
